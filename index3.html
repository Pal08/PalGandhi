Computer Aided Design

Fusion 360 · Parametric Modelling · 3D Sketching · Assembly & Motion

In this module I learned how professionals use Computer Aided Design (CAD) software to create precise parametric 3D models and engineering components. Using Fusion 360, I explored 2D sketching with constraints, solid 3D modelling, assemblies with joints, and completed a final design project — covering the full CAD workflow from concept sketch to finished model.

My Reflection

CAD is an entirely different discipline compared to web development or UI design. Precision is non-negotiable — every sketch line has a geometric relationship, every dimension drives the shape, and one unconstrained point can break an entire model.

Learning Fusion 360's constraint system was the biggest shift in my thinking. Once I understood how fully constrained sketches work — where the geometry is locked and predictable — modelling became fast and reliable. The progression from a 2D sketch to a full 3D solid using Extrude and Revolve was genuinely satisfying.

The assembly and joint session on Day 5 opened up a new dimension — watching a hinge mechanism move in simulation exactly as designed showed me the real power of parametric CAD. The final project tied everything together and gave me a workflow I can apply to real engineering problems.
